Problem 01 / 05

Climate Change

1.2°C above pre-industrialavg. global temperature rise since pre-industrial times
Current Situation

A Warming World

The Earth's average temperature has risen by approximately 1.2°C since pre-industrial times. The last decade was the hottest on record, with 2023 being the hottest single year ever measured. Arctic sea ice is declining at 13% per decade, and sea levels are rising at 3.6 mm per year. Extreme weather events are becoming more frequent and intense across every continent.

Root Causes

Fossil Fuels & Emissions

Burning coal, oil, and natural gas releases CO₂ and other greenhouse gases that trap heat in the atmosphere. Deforestation, industrial agriculture, cement production, and industrial processes contribute to the 37+ billion tonnes of CO₂ emitted globally each year. Transportation and energy production are the two largest sectoral sources worldwide.

Consequences

Cascading Disasters

More frequent and severe hurricanes, wildfires, droughts, and floods devastate communities globally. Glaciers and ice sheets are melting, threatening water supplies for billions. Entire island nations face submersion from rising seas. Crop failures and heat stress will displace hundreds of millions, intensifying global inequality and triggering unprecedented climate migration.

Solutions

The Path to Net Zero

Rapid transition to renewable energy — solar, wind, and hydro — while phasing out fossil fuels. Improving energy efficiency, electrifying transportation, protecting and restoring forests, and developing carbon capture technologies. Updating international climate agreements with enforceable targets and financing clean energy transitions in developing nations.

Problem 02 / 05

Deforestation

10 million hectares lostarea of forest cleared every single year worldwide
Current Situation

Forests Under Siege

An area of forest the size of Portugal is lost each year. The Amazon has already lost nearly 20% of its original cover and is approaching a tipping point beyond which it could transform into savannah permanently. Tropical forests — home to half of all land species — are disappearing fastest in Brazil, Indonesia, and the Congo Basin.

Root Causes

Agriculture & Industry

Agricultural expansion — cattle ranching, soy farming, and palm oil plantations — drives over 70% of tropical deforestation. Logging for timber and paper, mining, and infrastructure like roads and dams clear vast swaths yearly. Weak governance and corruption allow illegal clearing to continue unchecked despite international commitments to protect forests.

Consequences

Ecological Collapse

Forests regulate climate by absorbing CO₂ and controlling rainfall patterns. Their loss accelerates climate change and triggers regional droughts. Soil erosion, flooding, and loss of medicinal plants follow. Indigenous communities lose their ancestral homes. Over 80% of terrestrial biodiversity depends on forests, making deforestation a primary extinction driver.

Solutions

Protect, Restore, Transform

Expanding protected areas and empowering indigenous land stewards who have the best track record in forest conservation. Ending commodity-driven deforestation through supply chain regulations and sustainable certification. REDD+ payments reward developing nations for conservation. Large-scale reforestation and shifting diets toward less land-intensive foods reduce pressure.

Problem 03 / 05

Ocean Pollution

11 million tonnes of plasticenters the world's oceans every single year
Current Situation

Seas of Plastic & Toxins

The ocean absorbs 30% of global CO₂ and 90% of excess heat — yet it's under catastrophic stress. Over 5 trillion pieces of plastic float on the surface, forming vast garbage patches in ocean gyres. Dead zones — oxygen-depleted areas where marine life cannot survive — now number over 500 worldwide, covering more than 245,000 km².

Root Causes

Waste, Runoff & Industry

Single-use plastics, poorly managed landfills, and coastal dumping are the primary plastic sources. Agricultural fertilizer runoff causes algal blooms that create hypoxic dead zones. Industrial chemicals, pharmaceutical waste, heavy metals, and oil spills contaminate marine ecosystems. Noise pollution from massive shipping vessels disrupts whale communication and navigation.

Consequences

Marine Ecosystem Collapse

Sea turtles, seabirds, dolphins, and whales die from plastic ingestion and entanglement. Microplastics now permeate the entire food chain, including seafood consumed by billions of humans. Ocean acidification from CO₂ absorption dissolves coral reefs and shellfish shells. Coral bleaching has affected over 50% of the Great Barrier Reef.

Solutions

Clean & Protect

Global plastic reduction treaties with binding, enforceable targets. Redesigning packaging to be reusable or biodegradable by design. Improving waste management infrastructure in developing nations. Ocean cleanup technologies to remove existing debris. Reducing agricultural fertilizer runoff through precision farming. Expanding Marine Protected Areas to cover 30% of the ocean by 2030.

Problem 04 / 05

Biodiversity Loss

69% decline in wildlifeaverage population reduction since 1970
Current Situation

The Sixth Mass Extinction

Scientists warn we are in the sixth mass extinction — the first driven by a single species. Wildlife populations have declined 69% on average since 1970. One million species are currently threatened with extinction. Species are disappearing up to 1,000 times the natural background rate. We are losing biodiversity faster than science can even document it.

Root Causes

Habitat, Hunting & Climate

Habitat destruction from agriculture, urbanization, and deforestation is the leading driver. Overexploitation through hunting, fishing, and the illegal wildlife trade pushes species toward collapse. Invasive species introduced by humans outcompete native fauna. Pollution disrupts reproduction and immunity. Climate change shifts ecosystems faster than most species can migrate or adapt.

Consequences

Unraveling Ecosystems

Every extinction removes a node from the web of life, risking cascading collapse. Pollinators like bees support one-third of global food production — their decline directly threatens food security. Loss of predators destabilizes entire food chains. Fewer species means fewer medicines, less resilient agriculture, and diminished ecosystem services valued at trillions annually.

Solutions

Rewild & Restore

The 30x30 target — protecting 30% of land and ocean by 2030 — is a critical first step. Habitat connectivity corridors allow species to migrate safely. Rewilding — reintroducing apex predators and keystone species — revitalizes entire ecosystems. Ending wildlife trafficking through stronger enforcement. Reforming agricultural subsidies to reward biodiversity-friendly farming practices.

Problem 05 / 05

Air Pollution

7 million premature deathskilled by dirty air every year worldwide
Current Situation

The Invisible Killer

99% of the world's population breathes air that exceeds WHO safety guidelines. Air pollution kills approximately 7 million people prematurely each year — more than AIDS, tuberculosis, and malaria combined. South Asia and Sub-Saharan Africa bear the heaviest burden. Particulate matter (PM2.5) and nitrogen dioxide are among the most lethal pollutants worldwide.

Root Causes

Combustion & Industry

Burning fossil fuels for power, transport, and industry generates the majority of harmful emissions. Open burning of crops and biomass — still common in low-income nations for cooking and heating — releases massive amounts of toxic smoke. Vehicle exhaust, chemical manufacturing, construction dust, and wildfires intensified by climate change all contribute to degraded air quality.

Consequences

Health & Environment

Air pollution causes lung cancer, stroke, heart disease, respiratory infections, and COPD. Children exposed to polluted air suffer reduced cognitive development and lifelong health impacts. Acid rain from sulfur and nitrogen emissions devastates forests and acidifies lakes. Ground-level ozone damages crops globally. Economic losses from healthcare costs exceed $8 trillion annually.

Solutions

Clean Air for All

Transitioning to renewable electricity and electric vehicles eliminates the largest sources of pollution. Promoting clean cookstoves and fuels in developing nations could save millions of lives per decade. Stricter industrial emission standards with real-time monitoring and enforcement. Urban tree canopy programs filter particulates. International air quality frameworks enable cross-border cooperation and accountability.
